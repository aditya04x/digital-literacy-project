Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trustworthy source.

A common phishing scenario involves a student receiving an email that appears to be from their bank or a popular online service. The email usually contains an urgent message such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Verify your details immediately.” It also includes a link that directs the user to a fake website that looks almost identical to the real one.

When the student enters their login credentials on this fake website, the information is captured by the attacker. The attacker can then use these details to access the student’s bank account or personal accounts, leading to financial loss or identity theft.

Students and young individuals are often targeted because they frequently use online services and may not always verify the authenticity of such messages.

The consequences of phishing include loss of money, misuse of personal data, and unauthorized access to accounts. It can also cause stress and loss of trust in online platforms.
